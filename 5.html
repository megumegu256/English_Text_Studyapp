Tiny Houses テスト対策 🏠

全文ナビゲーション（クリックで該当箇所にジャンプ）

[1] In 1999, a man in the U.S. state of Wisconsin called Jay Shafer built a tiny house on wheels for himself. It had a floor area of just 10 square meters, yet he lived in it for five years. Later, he produced the first plans for tiny houses on wheels and went on to found companies that built them, as well as a society to promote this type of living. This gave birth to the Small House Movement. The timing for such a development was right. As the popularity of the minimalist approach to life shows, more and more people are becoming attuned to the idea of living more simply with fewer possessions. Living in a small house potentially offers a way to put the idea of simpler living into practice. [2] For Japanese people, who are accustomed to relatively small living spaces, it might come as a shock to discover that in 2021, the size of the average American family home was around 230 square meters. The small house movement advocated a return to houses of less than 93 square meters. Subsequently, the idea of the tiny house was developed. — one with a floor area of 37 square meters or less — [3] But for me at least, living in a tiny house is not an optimal solution. In opting to downscale my living accommodations so drastically, the first hurdle I would need to overcome is adapting to an extremely confined living space. For example, I would have to reduce my possessions to the absolute minimum and sacrifice my privacy. This simpler kind of lifestyle might suit retirees or couples whose children have grown up and moved out — so-called empty-nesters. For me, however, it is not an option, as I am married with two young children, and a family needs more space than a tiny house can provide. [4] Like Jay Shafer's original small home, some tiny houses are built on wheels, which means they can be considered as mobile homes and moved from place to place. However, for people who want to settle in one place in a tiny home, there are various obstacles. A tiny home may cost less to build than a conventional home, but the land on which it stands must also be purchased. And even if people can afford to do this, they may run into difficulties with zoning laws. In many places, tiny houses do not comply with construction regulations that specify the minimum size of a structure. [5] Nevertheless, people are continuing to come up with creative ways to get around such problems. Even though living in a tiny house is not a step I would take right now, I am grateful to the small house movement for making people reconsider what we need to lead a comfortable and fulfilling life.

本文 (Reading Passage)

Section 1

In 1999, a man in the U.S. state of Wisconsin called Jay Shafer

1999年、アメリカのウィスコンシン州に住むジェイ・シェイファーという名の男性が

built a tiny house on wheels for himself.

自分のために車輪付きの小さな家を建てました。

It had a floor area of just 10 square meters,

床面積はわずか10平方メートルでしたが、

yet he lived in it for five years.

それでも彼はそこに5年間住んでいました。

Later, he produced the first plans for tiny houses on wheels

後に、彼は車輪付きタイニーハウスの最初の設計図を作り、

and went on to found companies that built them,

それらを建てる会社を設立するに至りました、

as well as a society to promote this type of living.

さらにはこの種の暮らしを推進する団体も。

This gave birth to the Small House Movement.

これがスモールハウス・ムーブメントの誕生につながりました。

The timing for such a development was right.

そのような発展のタイミングは適切でした。

As the popularity of the minimalist approach to life shows,

ミニマリスト的な生活様式の人気が示すように、

more and more people are becoming attuned to the idea

ますます多くの人々がその考えに同調するようになっています

より少ない所有物でよりシンプルに暮らすという。

小さな家に住むことは、ある方法を潜在的に提供します

to put the idea of simpler living into practice.

よりシンプルな生活という考えを実践するための。

アメリカの平均的な家族の家の大きさが約230平方メートルだったと。

スモールハウス・ムーブメントは回帰を提唱しました

93平方メートル未満の家への。

Subsequently, the idea of the tiny house was developed.

その後、タイニーハウスという考えが生まれました。

— one with a floor area of 37 square meters or less —

ー床面積が37平方メートル以下のものですー

Section 3

But for me at least, living in a tiny house is not an optimal solution.

しかし、少なくとも私にとっては、小さな家に住むことは最適な解決策ではありません。

In opting to downscale my living accommodations so drastically,

住居をこれほどまでに抜本的に縮小することを選ぶ上で、

the first hurdle I would need to overcome is adapting

私が乗り越える必要がある最初の障害は適応することです

to an extremely confined living space.

極端に限定された居住空間に。

For example, I would have to reduce my possessions to the absolute minimum

例えば、私は持ち物を絶対的な最小限まで減らし、

and sacrifice my privacy.

そしてプライバシーを犠牲にしなければならないでしょう。

This simpler kind of lifestyle might suit retirees or couples

この種のよりシンプルなライフスタイルは、退職者や夫婦には合うかもしれません

whose children have grown up and moved out — so-called empty-nesters.

子供が成長して家を出た、いわゆる「空の巣族」の。

For me, however, it is not an option,

しかし私にとっては、それは選択肢ではありません、

なぜなら2人の幼い子供を持つ既婚者であり、

and a family needs more space than a tiny house can provide.

そして家族にはタイニーハウスが提供できる以上の空間が必要だからです。

Section 4

Like Jay Shafer's original small home, some tiny houses are built on wheels,

ジェイ・シェイファーの最初の小さな家のように、いくつかのタイニーハウスは車輪の上に建てられています、

which means they can be considered as mobile homes

これは、それらが移動式住居と見なされることを意味し、

and moved from place to place.

そして場所から場所へ移動できることを意味します。

However, for people who want to settle in one place in a tiny home,

しかし、タイニーホームで一箇所に定住したい人々にとっては、

there are various obstacles.

様々な障害があります。

A tiny home may cost less to build than a conventional home,

タイニーホームは従来の家よりも建設費用は安いかもしれませんが、

but the land on which it stands must also be purchased.

それが建つ土地も購入しなければなりません。

And even if people can afford to do this,

そして、たとえ人々がそれを買う余裕があったとしても、

they may run into difficulties with zoning laws.

彼らは地域指定法（都市計画法）で困難に直面するかもしれません。

In many places, tiny houses do not comply with construction regulations

多くの場所で、タイニーハウスは建築規制に従っていません

that specify the minimum size of a structure.

建物の最小サイズを定めた。

Section 5

Nevertheless, people are continuing to come up with creative ways

それにもかかわらず、人々は創造的な方法を考え出し続けています

to get around such problems.

そのような問題を回避するための。

Even though living in a tiny house is not a step I would take right now,

今すぐタイニーハウスに住むという一歩を踏み出すことはありませんが、

I am grateful to the small house movement

私はスモールハウス・ムーブメントに感謝しています

for making people reconsider what we need

私たちが必要なものを人々に再考させてくれたことに

to lead a comfortable and fulfilling life.

快適で充実した生活を送るために。
